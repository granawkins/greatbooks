# Iliad — Homer

## Source
- **URL**: https://classics.mit.edu/Homer/iliad.html
- **Translator**: Samuel Butler
- **Edition**: Internet Classics Archive (MIT)
- **Format**: HTML, one page per book (24 files)

## Structure
- 24 books (chapters)
- Butler's translation is prose, not verse — segments are sentences
- Groups correspond to prose paragraphs
- 4,836 total segments across all chapters

## Context
- **Composed**: ~8th century BCE (oral tradition, written down later)
- **Genre**: Epic poetry
- **Major themes**: Rage and honor (Achilles), mortality, the cost of war, fate vs. free will, the relationship between gods and mortals
- **Key characters**: Achilles, Hector, Agamemnon, Patroclus, Priam, Odysseus, Ajax, Paris, Helen, Zeus, Athena, Apollo
- **Note on translation**: Butler's prose translation (1898) uses Roman names for gods (Jove for Zeus, Minerva for Athena, etc.)
