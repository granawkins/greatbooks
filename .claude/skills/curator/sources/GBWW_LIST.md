# Great Books of the Western World — Complete List

**Source:** Wikipedia — "Great Books of the Western World"

## First Edition (1952) — 54 Volumes

### Volumes 1-3: Introductory
- Vol 1: The Great Conversation (introductory essay)
- Vol 2-3: Syntopicon (topical index of ideas)

### Vol 4 — Homer (Samuel Butler, prose)
- The Iliad
- The Odyssey

### Vol 5 — Greek Drama

- The Suppliant Maidens
- The Persians
- Seven Against Thebes
- Prometheus Bound

- The Eumenides

- Oedipus the King
- Oedipus at Colonus
- Antigone

- Electra

- Philoctetes

**Euripides** (Edward P. Coleridge, prose)
- Rhesus, Medea, Hippolytus, Alcestis, Heracleidae, The Suppliants, The Trojan Women, Ion, Helen, Andromache, Electra, Bacchantes, Hecuba, Heracles Mad, The Phoenician Women, Orestes, Iphigenia in Tauris, Iphigenia in Aulis, Cyclops

**Aristophanes** (Benjamin Bickley Rogers, verse)
- The Acharnians, The Knights, The Clouds, The Wasps, Peace, The Birds, The Frogs, Lysistrata, Thesmophoriazusae, Ecclesiazousae, Plutus

## Second Edition (1990) — Expanded to 60 Volumes

Updated translations for many existing works. Dropped: Apollonius's On Conic Sections, Sterne's Tristram Shandy, Fielding's Tom Jones, Fourier's Analytical Theory of Heat.

### Added pre-20th century works:
- **John Calvin** — Institutes of the Christian Religion (selections)
- **Erasmus** — The Praise of Folly
- **Moliere** — The School for Wives, Critique of the School for Wives, Tartuffe, Don Juan, The Miser, The Would-Be Gentleman, The Imaginary Invalid
- **Jean Racine** — Berenice, Phedre
- **Voltaire** — Candide
- **Denis Diderot** — Rameau's Nephew
- **Kierkegaard** — Fear and Trembling
- **Nietzsche** — Beyond Good and Evil
- **Tocqueville** — Democracy in America
- **Jane Austen** — Emma
- **George Eliot** — Middlemarch
- **Charles Dickens** — Little Dorrit
- **Mark Twain** — Huckleberry Finn
- **Honore de Balzac** — Cousin Bette
- **Henrik Ibsen** — A Doll's House, The Wild Duck, Hedda Gabler, The Master Builder

### New 20th century volumes (55-60):
**Vol 55 — Philosophy:** William James (Pragmatism), Henri Bergson, John Dewey, Alfred North Whitehead (Science and the Modern World), Bertrand Russell (The Problems of Philosophy), Martin Heidegger (What Is Metaphysics?), Ludwig Wittgenstein (Philosophical Investigations), Karl Barth

**Vol 56 — Science/Math:** Henri Poincare, Max Planck, Whitehead (An Introduction to Mathematics), Albert Einstein, Arthur Eddington, Niels Bohr, G. H. Hardy, Werner Heisenberg, Erwin Schrodinger, Theodosius Dobzhansky, C. H. Waddington

**Vol 57 — Economics:** Thorstein Veblen, R. H. Tawney, John Maynard Keynes

**Vol 58 — Social Science:** Sir James George Frazer, Max Weber, Johan Huizinga, Claude Levi-Strauss

**Vol 59 — Literature I:** Henry James (The Beast in the Jungle), George Bernard Shaw (Saint Joan), Joseph Conrad (Heart of Darkness), Anton Chekhov (Uncle Vanya), Luigi Pirandello (Six Characters in Search of an Author), Marcel Proust ("Swann in Love"), Willa Cather (A Lost Lady), Thomas Mann (Death in Venice), James Joyce (A Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man)

**Vol 60 — Literature II:** Virginia Woolf (To the Lighthouse), Franz Kafka (The Metamorphosis), D. H. Lawrence (The Prussian Officer), T. S. Eliot (The Waste Land), Eugene O'Neill (Mourning Becomes Electra), F. Scott Fitzgerald (The Great Gatsby), William Faulkner (A Rose for Emily), Bertolt Brecht (Mother Courage), Ernest Hemingway (The Short Happy Life of Francis Macomber), George Orwell (Animal Farm), Samuel Beckett (Waiting for Godot)
